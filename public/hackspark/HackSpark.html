HackSpark-2.0: Ignite Ideas, Build the Future

Aligned with the vision of the Institution's Innovation Council (IIC), HackSpark 2.0 is a 24-hour national-level hackathon organised by Thakur Shyamnarayan Engineering College (TSEC) in collaboration with InQubit, designed to give students hands-on exposure to innovation, problem-solving, and startup thinking.
Participants work in teams to brainstorm ideas, design solutions, and build functional prototypes within a fast-paced, collaborative environment. The hackathon encourages students to apply technical skills, creativity, and critical thinking to real-world challenges. High-potential teams will get an opportunity to receive mentorship and incubation support through InQubit, helping them further develop their ideas beyond the hackathon. A look back at HackSpark-1.0 and highlights.

HackSpark 1.0 ‚Äî Highlights

Under the banner of the IIC, a 24-hour Hackathon was successfully organized, offering a dynamic platform for students to explore AI-driven solutions to real-world problems faced by both public and private sectors. Participants were encouraged to develop innovative applications using machine learning, natural language processing, computer vision, automation, and intelligent decision-making systems. The event aimed to foster an innovation-driven culture, critical thinking, and collaborative problem-solving. Judging criteria included originality of ideas, technical soundness, presentation clarity, feasibility, and compatibility.

Mentors & Outcomes

HackSpark was supported by expert mentors who guided participants with technical insights and strategic feedback throughout the hackathon. The event led to innovative prototypes, enhanced problem-solving skills, and strengthened teamwork. Participants gained real-world experience in rapid development and pitching solutions.


About Us

HackSpark is powered to promote innovation, technical excellence, and entrepreneurship among students.

TSEC

Thakur Shyamnarayan Engineering College (TSEC) is a Hindi Linguistic Minority Institute under Zagdu Singh Charitable Trust, approved by All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE), recognized by the Government of Maharashtra, and affiliated with the University of Mumbai, where innovation meets expertise. Our institute stands as a beacon of excellence in engineering and technology education by conversion of a Diploma institute (Thakur Polytechnic- established in 1998) into a Degree Institute under Thakur Education Group (TEG)

IIC

The Institution's Innovation Council (IIC), established by the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) and the Ministry of Education's Innovation Cell (MIC), aims to foster a culture of innovation and entrepreneurship in higher education institutions (HEIs). It encourages students and faculty to engage in innovative thinking, problem-solving, and research activities. By establishing IICs in institutions, the goal is to create a dynamic and vibrant local ecosystem where innovation can flourish.

TPOLY

Thakur Polytechnic is affiliated to the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education (MSBTE) and conduct courses approved by the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE), New Delhi and recognized by Govt. of Maharashtra. It fulfils all the norms and standards set by the AICTE as well as the Maharashtra State Board of Technical Education. The Polytechnic was accorded approval by AICTE vide their letter no F-740-80-819-(F)/FT/98 dtd. 24th July 1998. Tpoly is a Hindi Linguistic Minority Institute. Thakur Polytechnic (Tpoly) was established in the year 1998.

InQubit

InQubit is a next-generation incubation and innovation centre designed to transform ideas into impactful, scalable solutions. We work at the convergence of technology, research, and entrepreneurship, enabling innovators to move from concept to execution. Our focus spans high-growth domains such as AI & ML, XR, Robotics, IoT, Drone & Aerospace, EV & Battery Systems, Embedded Technologies, Biotechnology, Sustainability, Defence Tech, and IT & SaaS. InQubit brings together students, startups, faculty, industry mentors, and investors to create a strong, collaborative innovation ecosystem.
